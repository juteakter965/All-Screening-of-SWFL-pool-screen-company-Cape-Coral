What is a Pool Screen Company?

A pool screen company specializes in designing, installing, repairing, and maintaining pool screen enclosures (also known as pool cages or lanais). These companies employ skilled technicians who understand the structural requirements, building codes, and best practices for creating durable, functional pool enclosures that withstand Florida's challenging weather conditions.

Professional pool screen companies in Cape Coral offer comprehensive services including new construction, repairs, rescreening, custom designs, and maintenance. They work with various screen materials, aluminum framing systems, and door configurations to create enclosures that meet your specific needs and comply with local building codes.

Services Offered by Pool Screen Companies

New Pool Screen Enclosure Installation

New installation involves complete design and construction of a pool screen enclosure from the ground up. This includes:

  • Site assessment and measurements
  • Custom design based on pool area and home architecture
  • Permitting and code compliance
  • Aluminum frame installation
  • Screen material installation
  • Door and entry system installation
  • Final inspection and cleanup

Screen Repair Services

Screen repairs address tears, holes, and damage to existing screen panels. Common repairs include:

  • Small tear patching
  • Individual panel replacement
  • Spline replacement and re-securing
  • Storm damage repairs
  • Pet damage repairs

Complete Rescreening

Rescreening involves replacing all screen material in an existing enclosure while keeping the aluminum frame structure intact. This is recommended when screens show widespread aging, fading, or multiple areas of damage.

Structural Repairs and Modifications

Pool screen companies also handle aluminum frame repairs, including:

  • Bent or damaged frame straightening
  • Post replacement
  • Beam and crossbar repairs
  • Door track and roller replacement
  • Structural reinforcement

Custom Design Services

Many pool screen companies offer custom design options including unique shapes, cathedral ceilings, multiple entry points, integrated lighting systems, and specialty screen materials for enhanced privacy or solar protection.

Pool Screen Enclosure Maintenance

Proper maintenance extends the life of your pool screen enclosure and prevents costly repairs. Follow these recommended maintenance practices:

Regular Cleaning Schedule

  • Monthly: Hose down screens to remove dust, pollen, and debris
  • Quarterly: Clean screens with mild soap solution and soft brush
  • Bi-Annually: Inspect all connections, splines, and frame components
  • Annually: Professional inspection and maintenance service

Storm Preparation

Hurricane Season Preparation

Before hurricane season, inspect your pool screen enclosure for any damage or weakness. Remove loose items from the pool area that could become projectiles. After storms, promptly address any damage to prevent further deterioration. Consider storm panels for additional protection in severe weather zones.

Damage Prevention Strategies

  • Trim nearby trees and bushes to prevent screen damage
  • Don't lean pool equipment against screens
  • Train pets not to scratch or jump on screens
  • Address small repairs immediately before they worsen
  • Keep pool chemicals away from aluminum frames
  • Use door closers to prevent slamming

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a pool screen enclosure last?

With proper maintenance, aluminum frames can last 20-30 years or more. Screen material typically needs replacement every 7-15 years depending on quality, weather exposure, and maintenance. Regular cleaning and prompt repairs extend screen life significantly.

Do I need a permit for pool screen enclosure work?

Yes, Cape Coral requires building permits for new pool screen enclosures and major structural modifications. Simple repairs and rescreening usually don't require permits. Reputable pool screen companies handle all permitting as part of their service.

What's the difference between pool screen companies and general contractors?

Pool screen companies specialize exclusively in screen enclosures and have specific expertise in aluminum framing, screen materials, and Florida's unique requirements. While general contractors can do pool screen work, specialists typically offer better quality, faster completion times, and deeper product knowledge.

How quickly can a pool screen company complete repairs?

Most small repairs (single panel replacement, small tears) can be completed same-day or within 24-48 hours. Complete rescreening typically takes 1-5 days depending on enclosure size. New installations require 3-10 days including permitting and inspections.

Is it worth repairing or should I replace my pool screen enclosure?

If the aluminum frame is structurally sound, rescreening is usually the cost-effective choice. Replace the entire enclosure when frames show significant corrosion, structural damage, or when the design no longer meets your needs. A professional pool screen company can assess your situation and recommend the best option.

What screen material should I choose for my pool enclosure?

Standard fiberglass screens work well for most applications. Consider solar screens if heat reduction is important, no-see-um screens for better insect protection, or pet-resistant screens if you have dogs or cats. Your pool screen company can recommend the best option based on your specific needs and budget.

Can pool screen enclosures withstand hurricane-force winds?

Pool screen enclosures are designed to meet Florida Building Code wind load requirements, but they're not intended to be hurricane barriers. Screens can tear in severe storms, but properly constructed frames should remain intact. Many homeowners add storm panels for additional protection during hurricanes.

How much does it cost to hire a pool screen company in Cape Coral?

Small repairs start around $75-$200. Individual panel replacements cost $150-$400. Complete rescreening ranges from $2,500-$10,000 depending on size. New enclosure installations typically cost $8,000-$30,000 based on size, design complexity, and materials. Always get multiple detailed quotes for comparison.

Does homeowners insurance cover pool screen damage?

Coverage depends on your policy and the cause of damage. Storm damage is often covered (minus deductible), while normal wear and tear or neglected maintenance typically isn't. Pet damage coverage varies by policy. Review your policy and consult your insurance agent to understand your specific coverage.

How do I maintain my pool screen enclosure?

Hose down screens monthly to remove debris. Clean with mild soap and soft brush quarterly. Inspect splines, frames, and connections bi-annually. Schedule professional maintenance annually. Address small repairs immediately. Keep vegetation trimmed away from the enclosure. Proper maintenance significantly extends the life of your pool screen.
